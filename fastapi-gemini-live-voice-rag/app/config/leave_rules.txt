UNIVERSITY OF PESHAWAR — LEAVE RULES 1977
(Reference document for the voice assistant. Answer questions about leave
policy using ONLY the information below. If something isn't covered here,
say you don't have that information rather than guessing.)

Preamble: These Rules revise leave admissible to University of Peshawar
employees. Title: University of Peshawar Leave Rules 1977. Commencement:
effective from 18.11.1976. Application: applies to all University employees;
deputationists follow their parent organization's leave rules unless their
service contract says otherwise.

KEY DEFINITIONS
- Pay: monthly amount drawn as substantive/officiating pay plus overseas,
  technical, special, or personal pay, or other Syndicate-classed pay.
- Employee: a person holding a post in University service, paid from
  University funds.
- Year: calendar year.
- Average Pay: rate of pay drawn immediately before leave on average pay
  commences.
- Leave Salary: monthly amount paid to an employee while on leave.
- Duty: period performing duties of a post, including probation/apprentice
  service (if followed by confirmation) and up to 3 months of authorized
  training, refresher courses, study tours, or exchange programmes.
- Heads of Departments: includes Chairmen of Teaching Departments,
  Directors of Institutes/Academies, Principals of constituent colleges,
  Registrar/Treasurer/Controller of Examinations, Principals of University
  Schools.
- Regular Vacations: Summer (12 weeks), Winter (2 weeks), Spring (2 weeks).

KINDS OF LEAVE
Privilege leave, Sick leave, Maternity leave, Study leave, Sabbatical
leave, Special Disability leave, Leave preparatory to retirement, Leave
not due, Extra Ordinary leave, Special leave.

PRIVILEGE LEAVE
Two kinds: (a) half average pay, earned at 1/12th of duty period; (b)
average pay, earned at rates depending on vacation entitlement — full
vacation staff earn 1 month per year of duty; partial-vacation staff earn
at 1/24th of duty period; non-vacational staff earn 4 days per calendar
month (duty of 15 days or less in a month is ignored, more than 15 days
counts as a full month). No reason needs to be given for taking due
leave. Accumulation is unlimited, but average-pay leave granted at one
time is capped at 6 months. Granting authority: Vice-Chancellor, who may
delegate to Heads of Departments.

SICK LEAVE
Granted on production of a medical certificate; debited against privilege
leave credit. Half-average-pay leave can be converted to average-pay
leave (2 days half-average = 1 day average) up to 12 months at a time.
Full pay sick leave up to 180 days at a time; with medical certificate,
up to 365 days total during entire service. Granting authority:
Vice-Chancellor (delegable).

MATERNITY LEAVE
Up to 3 months on full pay, timed so the delivery date falls within the
leave period and leave doesn't extend more than 6 weeks past delivery.
Can combine with other leave types. Not debited to leave account.
Granting authority: Vice-Chancellor (delegable).

STUDY LEAVE
For a permanent employee with at least 3 years of service, to pursue a
higher degree. Not granted if the employee would retire within 5 years
of the leave (plus any combined leave) ending. Maximum 3 years at a
time, 4 years total across a career (extendable to 4 years by converting
part to half-average pay: 2 days half = 1 day average). Counts as
service for increment/promotion/gratuity/bonus, not for leave. Requires
a bond: 6 months–1 year of leave requires 2 years of subsequent service;
1–2 years requires 3 years' service; 2–3+ years requires 5 years'
service. Breach penalty: Rs. 10,000 per year of shortfall. Amended rules
(1989): Ph.D up to 5 years total (3 years with pay, 2 without); M.Phil/LLM
2 years; M.Sc/M.A/M.S 1 year. Study leave rules effective from
27.7.1989.

SABBATICAL LEAVE
One year on average pay after every 6 years of active service, max 2
years total career. Only for research related to the teacher's subject,
with proof required. Cannot be taken mid-semester or combined with other
leave. Employee retains University housing if family continues residing
there.

EXTRA-ORDINARY LEAVE
Granted when no other leave is admissible, or by employee's written
request even if other leave is available. No leave salary is paid. Cap:
5 years leave without pay per each 10 years of continuous service; if
under 10 years of continuous service, max 2 years at the authority's
discretion. Granting authority: Vice-Chancellor (delegable).

SPECIAL DISABILITY LEAVE
For injury sustained in the course of official duty. Must be reported/
manifest within 3 months of the incident (exceptions possible). Max 24
months per occurrence, supported by medical certificate. Leave salary:
full average pay for first 4 months, then half average pay (or average
pay capped at what would otherwise be admissible). Counts as duty for
pension/gratuity purposes; only half is debited to leave account.

SPECIAL LEAVE
Syndicate may grant special leave to permanent employees with 3+ years
of service, on terms it deems fit. Female employees: up to 130 days full
pay on husband's death (not debited to leave account, needs death
certificate). Leave Ex-Pakistan: full pay, capped at Rs. 3000/month while
abroad, max 120 days at a time; leave salary rules depend on appointment
date. Death while in service: lump sum equal to full pay for up to 180
days of accrued leave, paid to the family. Permanent incapacitation
(Medical Board certified): 180 days of accrued leave paid, plus senior
post allowance during leave.

LEAVE PREPARATORY TO RETIREMENT (LPR)
Up to 12 months at retirement (age 60). If not availed, leave salary for
up to 180 days is payable instead — lump sum at retirement or monthly in
arrears. Granting authority: Vice-Chancellor.

LEAVE NOT DUE
Full pay leave offset against future earned leave, max 365 days across a
career, capped at 90 days within the first 5 years of service.

CASUAL LEAVE
Up to 25 days/year if not entitled to regular vacation; 10 days/year if
entitled to full regular vacation; 13 days/year if entitled to partial
regular vacation. Max 10 days casual leave at a time for those with the
25-day entitlement. Granting authority for Heads of Departments: Vice-
Chancellor; Heads may grant casual leave to their own staff.

GENERAL RULES
- Max leave on half average pay (or combined with average pay) at one
  time: 1 year, extendable to 2 years with a medical certificate.
- Conferences/seminars/meetings (including travel time) count as duty.
- Examination duty counts as duty, capped at 25 days per exam, one exam
  supervised per year, max 25 days absence per year for this purpose.
- Leave cannot be claimed as a right — the granting authority may refuse
  or revoke it based on service needs.
- Unauthorized absence forfeits pay for that period.
- A leave account is maintained for every employee.
- Temporary/officiating service counted for pension also counts for
  leave purposes.
- Non-permanent employees earn average-pay leave at 1/22nd of duty
  period (forfeited on service termination); half-average-pay medical
  leave for temporary staff: 15 days per completed year of service after
  1 year of continuous service.
- Resuming duty before leave expiry requires Vice-Chancellor approval.
- Accepting other employment during leave without prior sanction forfeits
  leave salary from that date.
